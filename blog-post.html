Nasdaq 100: Key Levels to Watch for the New York Open

By Ahmad Riezkih April 7, 2026 5 Min Read

Trading the Nasdaq requires more than just understanding price action; it requires understanding time and liquidity. As we approach the New York session, several key levels have formed that will dictate the direction of the trend.

The Liquidity Void

Last night’s Asian session left a significant fair value gap (FVG) between 18,150 and 18,180. In my experience, the market often seeks to "fill" these voids before continuing its primary move.

"Price doesn't move randomly; it moves from one area of liquidity to the next."

Strategic Execution

For today's session, I am looking for a sweep of the previous day's high followed by a displacement lower. If we see a shift in market structure on the 5-minute chart, that will be our signal to enter.
